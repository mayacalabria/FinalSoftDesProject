The heat maps above show how the given player or team differs from the league average, in either shooting percentage or shot location frequency. To get this visualization, we leveraged the fact that the NBA splits the court up into distinct shooting zones, which lets every (x,y) location be associated with one of these zones. To build our heat maps we wrote scripts to go through all of the data and calculate each player and team's shooting percentage and frequency by zone (or percentage of their total shots coming from a given zone); for each year the league averages for these values were subtracted from the player and team's values. These differences are what we used to associate zones with colors. Both types of heat map uses the same color scheme, where red means they are above the leage average, blue means they are below, and white means they are right in the middle. For a more exact view of how they compared to the league, just rest your curser over a given hexagon and you will be able to see the exact percentage difference.
